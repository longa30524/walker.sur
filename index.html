Oval Racing in the USA

A high-speed American tradition

What is Oval Racing?

Oval racing is a type of auto racing where drivers compete on oval-shaped tracks, typically with banked turns. It is one of the most popular forms of motorsport in the United States. The most famous series is NASCAR (National Association for Stock Car Auto Racing).

Famous Oval Tracks

  • Daytona International Speedway (Florida)
  • Indianapolis Motor Speedway (Indiana)
  • Talladega Superspeedway (Alabama)
  • Charlotte Motor Speedway (North Carolina)

NASCAR and IndyCar

NASCAR features stock cars racing at speeds over 200 mph. IndyCar, another American series, uses open-wheel cars and also competes on oval tracks, including the iconic Indy 500.

Why Americans Love Oval Racing

The simplicity of the track, high-speed action, and close competition make oval racing thrilling for fans. Millions watch live and attend races annually.
